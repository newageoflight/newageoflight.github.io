Hyperthyroidism/thyrotoxicosis

Hypothyroidism/myxoedema

Questions box
  1. Hx thyroid problems?
  2. FHx thyrotoxicosis? (Graves', other autoimmune diseases)
  3. Amiodarone/thyroxine use?
  4. I2 exposure? (e.g. radiocontrast use in those w/ existing multinodular goitre)
  5. (!!!) Palpitations (AF at presentation)
  6. Insomnia/irritability/Hactivity?
  7. Wt loss, ch'd bowel habits, inc'd sweating, heat intolerance?
  8. Mm weakness (proximal muscle weakness - may have noticed difficulty getting out of chair)
  9. Eye problems e.g. double vision, grittiness, redness, pain behind eyes
  1. Have you found cold weather more difficult to cope with recently?
  2. Constipation
  3. Wt gain
  4. Have you noticed skin becoming dry?
  5. Memory not as good as before? Depression?
  6. Hoarseness? (hTH speech; 1/3 pts)
  7. Leg swelling?

General inspection
  • Wt loss
  • Anxiety/fidgeting, hyperactivity
  • Frightened facies
  • Obvious mental and physical sluggishness
  • Myxoedema madness
  • hTH speech (slow, nasal, deep)

Hands
  • Fine tremor on extension (Sy overactivity - may be easier to see if a sheet of paper overlaid on pt's fingers)
  • Nails
    • Onycholysis (Plummer's nails <- Sy overactivity)
    • Clubbing (thyroid acropathy in Graves')
  • Palms
    • Palmar erythema
    • Warmth/sweatiness in palms (Sy overactivity)
  • Pulse
    • Sinus tachycardia (Sy overdrive)
    • AF (short refractory period of atrial cells <- Sy drive, hormone induced chs)
    • Collapsing character (high CO)
  • Peripheral cyanosis
  • Skin swelling (may also be cool and dry)
  • Palms
    • Palmar yellow discolouration (HCarotenm)
    • Palmar crease pallor (anaemia <- chronic disease, folate deficiency from bacterial overgrowth/B12 deficiency from pernicious anaemia, Fe deficiency from menorrhagia)
  • Pulse (may be slow, small volume)
  • Sensory loss <- carpal tunnel thickening

Arms
  • Test for proximal myopathy using shoulder power
  • Arm reflexes (abnormal briskness, esp in relaxation)
  • Peripheral myopathy (rare)
  • 'Hung-up' biceps or Achilles tendon reflex

Face
  • Eyes
    • Exophthalmos (look from in front, at sclerae and behind over forehead)
    • Complications of proptosis
      • Chemosis (conjunctival oedema + scleral injection, esp over insertion of lateral rectus)
      • Conjunctivitis
      • Corneal ulceration (<- can't close eyelids)
      • Optic atrophy (rare, maybe <- CNII stretching)
      • Ophthalmoplegia (IR power lost first - convergence weakened later)
    • Thyroid ophthalmopathy (<- Sy overactivity, not Graves' specific)
      • Thyroid stare (frightened expr'n)
      • Lid retraction (Dalrymple's sign = sclera visible sup iris)
      • Lid lag (von Graefe's sign)
        • Follow my finger - descent it at moderate rate from upper to lower visual field
        • Upper lid descent noticeably lags behind that of eyeball
    • Ptosis (if you see it, rule out MG)
  • Sweating
  • Skin/hair changes
    • Yellow skin (HCarotenm)
    • General thickening of skin
    • Cool, dry skin and hair
    • Thinning of scalp hair
    • Alopecia
    • Vitiligo
  • Eyes
    • Periorbital oedema
    • Outer third eyebrow loss
    • Xanthelasmata
  • Tongue swelling
  • hTH speech (if not already done)
  • Bilateral sensorineural deafness

Neck
  • Thyroid enlargement (detectable 60-90%)
    • Graves
      • Classically diffusely enlarged, smooth, firm
      • Ass'd thrill usu rpesent, but not specific for Graves' thyrotoxicosis
      • No enlargement = unlikely to be Graves
    • Thyrotoxicosis without Graves
      • Toxic multinodular goitre
      • Solitary nodule (toxic adenoma)
      • Painless/postpartum/subacute (de Quervain's) thyroiditis
        • de Quervain typically mod enlarged, firm, tender
    • Thyrotoxicosis w/o any goitre
      • Elderly pt
      • HTHm <- rare trophoblastic tissue abnormality e.g. hydatidiform mole, testis/uterus choriocarcinoma, xs TH replacement
  • Thyroidectomy scars
    • hPTHm
      • Chvostek's sign
      • Trousseau's sign
  • Other skin changes e.g. erythema

Goitre (compensatory TSH oversecretion due to TH decrease when viable tissue still exists, in severe I2 deficiency, enzyme deficiency (congenital), late Hashimoto, treated thyrotoxicosis)
  • Tracheal deviation
  • When assessing the thyroid, look for
    • Size
    • Symmetry
    • Consistency
      • Masses
      • Site
      • Shape
      • Tenderness
      • Consistency
      • Mobility
    • Palpable thrill
    • See “gland-only exam” for the rest
  • LN
    • Supraclavicular
    • Anterior cervical
    • Posterior cervical
    • Submental

Chest
  • Gynaecomastia (occasionally)
  • Systolic murmurs (<- inc'd CO)
  • CCF signs (precipitated by thyrotoxicosis in elderly)
  • Pericardial effusion
  • Pleural effusion

Legs
  • Pretibial myxoedema (2lat firm, elev'd dermal nodules/plaques - pink/brown/skin colour <- mucopolysaccharide accumulation - only in Graves')
  • Proximal myopathy (25%)
  • Hyperreflexia (25%)
  • Non-pitting oedema
  • Kneel on a chair with exposed ankles
  • Achilles tendon reflex (normal contraction, delayed relaxation)
  • Signs of peripheral neuropathy
  • Other neurological associations
