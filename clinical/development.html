    Less music/dance
    dancers on the lawn
    Flat or negative vocal and facial affect
    guy passed out in front of the door

  • Kohlberg's model of moral reasoning (what determines right/wrong for the child?) (walk: Matthews Tute/CATS Rooms)
    Pre-conventional
    the room is full of people race-typing registration forms (CATS 301)
    1. Punishment :: the front of the room is showing BDSM
    2. Naïve reward (what's in it for me?) :: rear wall has a mural of raining money
    Conventional
    partying people (a bit like AMSA) (CATS 302)
    1. Good boy/girl (close others' approval) :: front of the room shows Shirley Temple singing "On the good ship lollipop"
    2. Authority (rigid adherence to societal rules/laws) :: the Queen of England standing at the back of the room
    Post-conventional
    lots of passed out drunken people (CATS 303)
    1. Social contract (societal rules, but these are fallible rather than absolute) :: Thomas Hobbes' Leviathan at the room front
    2. Individual principles and conscience (abstract ethics emphasising equity, justice) :: US founding fathers at the back - the back wall has basically become Mt Rushmore

3 20: Normal childhood development

3.1 Redo (spare)

  • 6w
    • Gross motor
      • Resolution of primitive reflexes in 1st 4-6mo
      • Lifts head in prone
    • Eye/hand
      • Hands usually open
      • Visually follows light or red ring horizontally and vertically
      • Grasps objects placed in hand
    • Hearing/speech
      • Vocalises in social response
      • Quietens and "listens" to meaningful sounds
      • 4 basic cries
    • Social/self-help
      • Smiles in social response
      • Visually recognises mother
  • 3mo
    • Gross motor
      • Supine
      • good head control on traction.
      • Some head wobble in supported sitting.
      • Back rounded in supported sitting
      • Prone
      • head and chest up, supports self on forearms
    • Eye/hand
      • Will hold rattle if put in hand
      • Hands together in midline & to mouth
      • Follow red ring
    • Hearing/speech
      • Laughs aloud
      • A lot of vocalisation
      • Searches for source of sound with eyes
      • Cooing - vowel sounds, few guttural sounds
    • Social/self-help
      • Laughs aloud
      • Friendly with strangers and familiars
      • Enjoys both and caring routines
      • Expresses 2+ emotions
      • Turns head to person talking
  • 6mo
    • Gross motor
      • Supine
      • firm head control and straight back in supported sitting
      • Rolls front to back
      • Prone
      • lifts head and chest well up on extended arms
      • Bears weight when held standing
    • Eye/hand
      • Reaches out for toys
      • Grasps toy with palmar grasp
      • Transfers toy from one hand to another
      • Raking grasp
    • Hearing/speech
      • Babbling - single syllables and strings of repetitive babble (e.g. babababa)
      • Babbles with sing-song tones and a lot of different sounds
      • Localises sounds by head turning left and right
    • Social/self-help
      • Shows selective repsonse to mother and familiars
      • Feeds self with biscuit, rusk etc.
      • Stretches to be picked up
  • 9mo
    • Gross motor
      • Sits alone well with lateral propping
      • Average of sitting: 7mo (rng 6-9mo)
      • May start to crawl (7-12mo)
      • May pull to stand (7-12mo)
    • Eye/hand
      • Uses thumb and index finger to pick up small objects in scissor fashion (lateral pincer)
      • Pokes at small objects with index finger
      • Finger opposition
      • Looks for fallen toy
      • Finds partially hidden toy
    • Hearing/speech
      • Listens to adults conversation
      • Babbles in double-syllables (mama, dada etc.) + jargon
      • Imitates adults' sounds and lip mvts (echolalia)
      • Localises sounds above and below ear level
      • May understand a few words and respond to "no"
    • Social/self-help
      • Puts hands around bottle or cup when feeding
      • Takes objects to mouth
      • Distinguishes strangers from familiars
      • Imitates hand-clapping and waving "ta-ta"
  • 12mo
    • Gross motor
      • Crawls well (or shuffles on bottom)
      • Pulls to standing on furniture
      • Walks sideways around furniture
      • May walk alone
    • Eye/hand
      • Pick up small objects with precise "pincer" grasp
      • Throws toys deliberately
      • Able to release object from grasp voluntarily
      • Hold pencil to mark paper
    • Hearing/speech
      • Knows and turns to own name
      • Imitates adult's vocalisation
      • Long strings of inflected babble
      • First single words
      • Demonstrates "definition by use" of common object e.g. brush
    • Social/self-help
      • Finds toys hidden while he is watching
      • Demonstrates affection to familiars
      • Plays Pat-a-Cake and waves "bye-bye"
  • 15mo
    • Gross motor
      • Walks alone with wide base, using arms for balance
      • Crawls upstairs on all 4's
      • Push a trolley/pram
    • Eye/hand
      • Builds tower of 2 blocks in imitation
      • Holds crayon in whole-handed grasp
      • Imitates linear scribble
      • Point with index finger at distant objects
    • Hearing/speech
      • Has 3-4 meaningful words
      • Understands many more
      • Obeys simple instructions
      • "Talks" in strings of speech like sounds ("jargon")
    • Social/self-help
      • Holds and drinks from a cup
      • Holds spoon but spills
      • Helps with dressing
      • Very active in exploring environment
      • Plays in close vicinity of mother or familiar
  • 18mo
    • Gross motor
      • Walks pulling toy on string
      • Walks upstairs 2 feet/step with one held
      • Throws ball
      • Stoop and recover
      • Trotting
    • Eye/hand
      • Blocks: tower of 3
      • Crayon: purposeful linear scribble, may have single hand dominance
    • Hearing/speech
      • "Jargon"
      • Many words (~50)
      • Start 2 word combinations
      • Points to 2 parts of body (hair, nose, etc.)
    • Social/self-help
      • Feeds self with spoon
      • Handles cup well
      • Clean if taken to the toilet regularly
      • Start of parallel play
  • 2y
    • Gross motor
      • Goes upstairs alone
      • Walks backwards
      • Runs
      • Stands to kick ball
    • Eye/hand
      • Blocks
      • tower of 6-7
      • imitates "train" without chimney
      • Crayon
      • circular scribble in imitation
      • imitates vertical stroke
      • unscrew lid of small bottle
    • Hearing/speech
      • Asks for common things by name
      • Points to 4 parts of body
      • Joins 2-3 words in short sentences
      • 50-200 words
      • Follow instructions with 2 key words
      • Attention single channeled
    • Social/self-help
      • Takes off socks and shoes
      • Tries actively to dress and undress
      • Asks for toilet or pot - clear and dry by day
      • Plays near, but not with, other children
      • Early symbolic play e.g. dolls
      • 2.5y
    • Gross motor
      • Jumps off floor
      • Stand on 1 foot for a few seconds
      • Jump from 1 step
    • Eye/hand
      • Blocks
      • tower of 8
      • Imitates "train" with chimney
      • Pencil
      • imitates vertical and horizontal strokes
      • Replaces screw-cap of small bottle
      • Single hand preference
    • Hearing/speech
      • "Jargon" almost disappeared
      • Short sentences - 4+ syllables
      • Knows first name
      • Answer simple questions
    • Social/self-help
      • Indicates need for toilet
      • Takes off pants
      • Assist in simple household tasks
      • Start of cooperative play
  • 3y
    • Gross motor
      • Goes upstairs one foot/step
      • Pedals tricycle
      • Walks on tiptoes
    • Eye/hand
      • Blocks
      • tower of 9+
      • imitates bridge
      • Pencil
      • mature pencil grip
      • copies circle
      • Uses scissors
    • Hearing/speech
      • Talks in sentences 6 syllables
      • Ask questions "what", "where"
      • Uses pronouns
      • Follows instructions with 3 key words
      • Shift attention focus
      • Rote count to 4
      • Understand big/little
    • Social/self-help
      • Washes and dries hands
      • Dresses himself except for buttons and laces
      • Undoes buttons
      • Knows own gender
      • Plays cooperatively
  • 4y
    • Gross motor
      • Hops on one foot
      • Run to kick a ball
      • Jump from 2 steps
    • Eye/hand
      • Blocks
      • copies 6 block pyramid
      • Pencil
      • copies cross
    • Hearing/speech
      • Talks in compound sentences - joins sentences with "because", "but if"
      • Asks questions "why"
      • Enjoys telling stories and jokes
      • Understand abstract language and more complex instructions
      • Names few colours
      • Count correctly to 4
    • Social/self-help
      • Can do up front buttons
      • Participates in imaginative group play
      • Give family name
  • 5y
    • Gross motor
      • Broad jump
      • Walk downstairs 1 foot/step
    • Eye/hand
      • Pencil
      • copy square/few letters
      • draw house
      • Cut strip of paper with scissors
    • Hearing/speech
      • Longer sentences (10 syllables)
      • Understand opposites, composition
      • Count correctly to 10
    • Social/self-help
      • Dress, undress + socks, shoes (not laces)
      • Brush teeth
  • 6y
    • Gross motor
      • Run upstairs
      • Skip
      • Bounce and catch ball
    • Eye/hand
      • Builds 10 block stairs
      • Write 1st name
      • Copy triangle and maybe diamond
    • Hearing/speech
      • Understand similarities and differences
    • Social/self-help
      • Know address (2 parts)
      • 1 special friend

        • Hop on one foot (4y)

      • Legends
