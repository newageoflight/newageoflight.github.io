2.1. Taking a D&A history

  • This is probably not 100% correct because it’s just based on notes I’ve read from SA Health (AOD) and personal experience (and getting shit on (albeit rather kindly) by the D&A boss during a night call)
  • D&A may expect you to know about all of the following major classes
    • Depressants
      • Alcohol
      • Benzodiazepines
      • gamma-Hydroxybutyrate (GHB)
    • Stimulants
      • Amphetamines
      • Nicotine (tobacco)
    • Opioids
      • Heroin
      • Also others like oxycodone, buprenorphine, methadone
    • The other ones are generally more “annoying” than “deadly” as far as withdrawal symptoms go
      • Cannabis
      • Empathogens (e.g. MDMA)
      • Dissociatives (e.g. ketamine)
      • Hallucinogens (e.g. LSD, shrooms)
  • The qualifiers for each drug more or less mirror those for actual prescription drugs
    • Type
    • Route of administration
    • Quantity
      • Quantifiers can be a bit tricky for different types of drugs e.g. cannabis (how much is a cone?), amphetamines (“points” = multiples of 0.1g)
    • Frequency
    • Pattern of usage
      • Continuous vs intermittent
      • Specific timings e.g. weekends, time of day
      • Binging
    • Duration
      • First use
      • Most recent use (when?)
      • Factors associated with use/relapse
      • Periods of use and abstinence
    • Withdrawal
      • Any signs ATOR?
      • During past abstinence
      • Severity
      • Management
      • Reasons for resuming use (did the withdrawal push them to?)
      • Sequelae (esp for alcohol, benzos)
      • Delirium
  • Specific points
    • For methadone and some others you may want to know
      • How do they get it?
      • Who dispenses it and when?
  • Now that you have the “organiser” headings, how do you fill in the boxes?
    • As we always do, ask open-ended, non-judgemental questions
      • What do you like about X?
      • What are the positive experiences of using X?
      • What purpose does your substance use have for you?
      • What don’t you like about your substance use?
      • Does anyone else have concerns? If so, what do you think those concerns are?
      • How would you describe yourself when using X?
      • How would others (can insert a specific friend/family member) describe you when you are using substances?
      • Have you ever thought about or considered stopping/reducing your substance use? If so, tell me about the strategies you have considered to either reduce or stop your use
      • Ask the client to imagine a period of time (dd/ww/lifestyle) w/o their drug of choice - what would this be like?
      • Can you tell me about a time when you have thought of less harmful ways of using substances e.g. controlled drinking, safer use of other drugs (e.g. not injecting), not sharing needles/equipment, etc.
        • Can provide an opportunity to talk about harm reduction
  • If patients mention choices that reduce harm, provide positive feedback!
